Serious Game Design Based on First Aid Theme

I. Abstract

The purpose of this paper is to study the development of serious games and try to design a serious game. The concept of serious game was proposed by educational and sociologist Clark.C.Albert in 1970, and its kernel extension as well as application has been developed in the 21st century. This paper adopts a problem-based design approach to develop serious games by summarizing relevant theories and using the Unity engine as the core tool. This paper summarizes the development history of the concept of serious games at home and abroad, and finds that there is a lack of serious games with the background of medical topics in China. Therefore, this paper designs a serious game in which the audience plays the role of a 120 dispatcher through the waterfall development method, so as to popularize the related emergency knowledge.

II.The background of the selected topic

Serious Games (SG) refers to games that are not played for the primary purpose of entertainment, enjoyment or fun. The term appeared in 1970 in a book of the same name by educational and sociologist Clark.C.Albert, which states that â€œSerious games can be serious or casual. The significance of our focus on serious games is that they have a clear, carefully thought-out educational purpose and are not used primarily for entertainment. This does not mean that serious games are not entertaining, nor should they be1.â€ And in 2002, Ben Sawyer published a white paper, Serious Games: Improving Public Policy through Game-Based Learning and Simulation, calling for the use of technology and knowledge from the entertainment video game industry to improve the behavior of public organizations that simulate the real world in a game framework. In the same year, Americaâ€™s Army, a game designed by Redstone Arsenalâ€™s Army Game Studio to encourage youth to join the military, was released, sparking widespread discussion in the industry, and these two landmark events made 2002 the beginning of the modern wave of serious games.2 However, so far, serious games have not been recognized by academics as the most important game in the world.

But so far, serious games do not have a strict definition in the academic world, different researchers and developers do not understand and define serious games in exactly the same way, and the more widely recognized essential feature at present is games that do not aim at entertainment. â‘¡ Serious games, as a branch of video games, should be able to have a certain educational ability, while retaining the characteristics of video games, so that the audience to acquire a certain skill or obtain certain knowledge.

III. Significance of the selection

First of all, Serious Games is not a game of pure entertainment. Serious Games research is an interdisciplinary field involving game design, education, psychology, communication and other aspects. With the development of computer technology, serious games are gradually expanding from the military field to other fields, such as health care, education and corporate training. The genres of serious games are very diverse, including simulation games, role-playing games, strategy games, and puzzle games. In addition, they can also be used on different platforms, such as computers, cell phones, tablets and virtual reality devices.
Currently, there is no strict definition of serious games in China and there is a lack of serious game works. In terms of definition, Li Fangli, in her research, classifies functional games into â€œstrong functional gamesâ€ and â€œweak functional gamesâ€ according to the different coordinate scales of â€œentertainment-functionalityâ€. â€œWeak Function Gamesâ€. Weak function games are more inclined to entertainment, more indirectly related to real problems, and achieve the purpose of improving general knowledge and cultivating sentiment through subtle ways. Functional games in the above definition are related to serious games, and the common feature of both is that they have the design purpose of improving the general knowledge of the audience3 .
Based on the above and related literature, this report tries to propose and create a serious game with a 120 emergency center dispatcher as the game character, in which the audience will answer the phone calls, reply to the messages in time, and save different patients in the city. An attempt to inject fresh blood into serious games in the country.

IV. Content of the game:

(i) Introduction of the case

The socio-educational significance of serious games has always been in the spotlight; Alessandro De Gloria et al. found that serious games are designed to provide engaging, motivating, and effective vehicles to improve the learning process, and that their engaging plots and interactions offer the pedagogical potential of efficiently acquiring and validating knowledge and skills in the audience.4 Wouters et al. experimentally demonstrated that serious games can improve learning by altering cognitive processes and influencing motivation. Wouters and other researchers have experimentally demonstrated that serious games can enhance learning by altering cognitive processes and influencing motivation.5 In China, Tang, Jinyu, and Zhu, have shown that serious games can improve learning by altering cognitive processes and influencing motivation.
Domestically, Tang Jinyu and Zhu Xuefang, in their study on serious games and digital non-genetic inheritance, also pointed out that serious games have the advantages of communication and cooperation mechanism, trial and error and immediate feedback mechanism, visual expression of information, interactivity and entertainment, which enable users to change from passive to active in receiving information and creating knowledge.6 In addition, old science and technology workers at Tsinghua University have shown that serious games can enhance learning through altering cognitive processes and influencing learning motivation through experiments.
In addition, the Association of Elderly Science and Technology Workers of Tsinghua University introduced the concept of medical and health serious games earlier in China and designed a series of works. Medical health serious game is the combination of virtual reality technology and medical technology to dissolve into the electronic game system, to realize convenient and natural human-computer interaction and realistic experience, so that it may achieve the purpose of assisting the treatment of diseases. This series of software treatment cost cheap, efficacy, the use of non-invasive intervention therapy, no obvious side effects, at home and abroad are in a leading position in technology, with great prospects for development . However, there is still a lack of serious games with the background of first aid in China, so this report also tries to design a serious game with the relevant background.

Raph Koster mentioned in â€œThe Way to Happy Game Designâ€ that the human brain is a marvelous pattern recognition machine, and we are good at discovering hidden patterns . Life Beacon removes the mouse from the operation level, i.e., the audience understands the core operation of the game through the button prompts before entering the game. Serious games actually face a number of challenges in their development: the first challenge is how to examine the reasonableness of the topic selection, followed by how to integrate educational themes and objectives into serious games once they have been determined so that they are both entertaining and serve an educational purpose, and how to assess the effectiveness of the learning as well as the fun of the game is another key issue to be solved for serious games.

(ii) Game Setting and Operation

The game designed for this report is called â€œLife Beaconâ€, in which the audience plays the role of a 120 operator, answering calls and selecting the correct dialog options through key combinations. There is a time limit for each missed call, which challenges the audienceâ€™s quick response. At the same time, the audience needs to screen out nuisance calls and hang up immediately to respond to other emergency calls, and in addition, the audience needs to have a certain degree of knowledge about first aid in order to deliver an effective message before the ambulance arrives. Through this game, the audience can empathize with the 120 operators, experience the urgency of their work, and learn about medical emergencies in the process of answering calls over and over again. The audience needs to answer as many emergency calls as possible within the time constraints to cope with different accidents and increase the value of life. If the audience answers the phone negatively, gives wrong answers or refuses to answer the phone, their life value will be reduced and the game will end prematurely.
In order to lower the threshold of the game and reduce the learning cost of the audience, Life Beacon chooses simple QTE (Quick Time Event) and story reading comprehension as the core gameplay, which only requires four arrow keys from up, down, left, right and right from entering the game to actual operation.

(iii) Design objectives

During the gameplay of Life Beacon, the audience needs to have a certain level of comprehension of the phone text as well as sufficient knowledge of first aid. Misdirection of the patient by the audience can cost the audience a significant amount of life value, and tangling with nuisance calls can waste a significant amount of time. Because of this feedback, the effectiveness of learning is tied to the goal of the game, as the amount of time the audience persists in the game depends, in part, on his mastery of the knowledge.
Beacon of Lifeâ€™s educational attributes distinguish it from the technical training field of conventional serious games, which tends to be public service in nature. Life Beacon not only publicizes and popularizes the knowledge in this field to fill the knowledge gap of the general public, but also raises the publicâ€™s respect for medical workers.

  1. Tools used
    The tools used for the development of this report include a variety of types of tools, the following will focus on Unity, Spine, Blender:

    • Unity: Unity is a cross-platform game engine software, which can be used to develop various types of games, including PC games, mobile games, virtual reality and augmented reality games, etc. Unity provides a series of tools and resources, such as graphic editors, audio editors, animation editors, physics engines, scripting engines, etc., which make game development easier.
    • Spine: Spine is a popular skeletal animation software for creating 2D animations.Spine provides a variety of skeletal and animation editing tools that allow users to easily create a variety of 2D animations and interactive effects.
    • Blender: Blender is an open source 3D computer graphics software for creating, editing and rendering 3D images and animations. blender provides a variety of modeling, animation, rendering and compositing tools that allow users to easily create a variety of complex 3D images and animations.

  2. Design process
    The members of this report group used xml language to write a large number of dialog texts in the game, and each event tag was inserted with a piece of dialog and numbered to facilitate direct configuration. In addition, the members in charge of the program were divided into two parts: one was in charge of the table guide, dealing with the logic of the events, and determining the win/loss conditions and the current state of the audience, while the other student, as the â€œfront-endâ€ developer, dealt with the visual effects of the audience interactions, as well as the position and animation state of the elements of the main interface of the game.
    In practice, many events can enter the same ending branch, including the plot branch and the impact branch (affecting the audienceâ€™s life value, news broadcasting), which saves a lot of text volume and development time. After further iterations, the text tool evolved into a fixed number of templates, which can be selected according to the importance of the plot, eliminating the need for separate designs.
